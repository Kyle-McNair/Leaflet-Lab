High Rises in North America

Click on any of the given cities to see more details
Cities in North America display from little, to hundreds of high rise buildings. While there is no distinct classification of how tall a skyscraper is, the universal height to be considered a "skyscraper" is over 100 meters (~330 feet). This map shows a time series of the amount of high rise structures built in each decade. The time spans from the 1890s, all the way to the 2010s. You will find New York to be the dominant home of skyscrapers, but also see other cities build dozens as time passes.
